UX research of rebranding

Task description

Research the key user flow after rebranding, get information from users about the new style.

Challenges

  1. Check to see if the rebranding will cause usability degradation.
  2. Gather feedback from respondents on the new style.
  3. Check both desktop and mobile versions.

Audience

As respondents, we invited project participants who had recently joined the team, as well as company employees who were not part of this project. This is a target audience, because the main audience of the site is IT specialists looking for a job. We tried to select people from different spheres — DEV, BA, QA, etc. Also the use of internal resources allowed us to conduct a study in a short time.

Conducting moderated research

First, we conducted a moderated study and asked them to perform a key action in one of the scenarios. The study was conducted remotely, with a screen demonstration by the respondents. We made 2 versions of interactive prototypes — desktop and mobile. Research was carried out for each version separately. The task sounded like «Try to find a vacancy for yourself and respond to it.»We asked for comments on actions, thoughts, impressions, doubts and pains during the process. We also asked additional questions during and at the end of the meeting. In addition to usability testing, we also asked respondents about the new design. As a result, we have collected additional comments on the rebranding. Later, some comments formed the basis of edits.
Two designers were present at each testing session. One communicated with the respondent, the second recorded insights. Thus, we saved time on transcribing the recording of the meeting.

Analysis of results

We conducted 10 testing sessions. The collected insights were divided into groups. We found that the rebrand did not negatively affect the user experience. All respondents successfully reached the end of the scenario. In addition, we received a few more insights to improve usability, which we left for further study. In the course of observations, we finalized the prototype as users interact with it. This helped to make a qualitative and complete prototype for further quantitative research.

Conducting a quantitative study

After the qualitative study, we conducted a quantitative study to test the findings with a larger sample of respondents. To do this, we uploaded an interactive prototype to Maze. We found respondents for this stage using the email mailing list. All conclusions from the qualitative study were confirmed.

Results

  • We found out that the rebranding did not affect the key user flow and users successfully completed the task. Therefore, rebranding will not worsen the main metrics after the release.
  • The rebranding was mostly positive for the respondents.
  • The most frequent comments on the visual formed the basis of rebranding improvements.
After completing the research and making improvements, we moved on to redesign the platform and the design system.
